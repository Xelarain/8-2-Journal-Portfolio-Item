# 8-2-Journal-Portfolio-Item
How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating "user stories" help with this?
When I'm trying to interpret user needs, I try to completely step back from the code and just put myself in the client's shoes. Writing user stories was honestly a game-changer for this. Using that specific "As a [user], I want to [action] so that [benefit]" format forced me to think about the actual, real-world value of a feature. It stops you from going down a rabbit hole of building cool but ultimately useless technical features, and keeps the whole team focused on solving the user's actual problem.

How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?
My whole approach to development has shifted. I used to think you had to map out an entire massive project from start to finish before starting, but now I'm all about taking things one step at a time. Honestly, juggling my coursework alongside working two jobs right now to save up for my move to Vegas has made me really appreciate the Agile mindset. Breaking massive, overwhelming tasks into short, focused Sprints and just working through a prioritized Product Backlog makes everything so much more manageable. When you build software in smaller chunks like that, it’s incredibly easy to pivot and adapt when requirements inevitably change or a roadblock pops up.

What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?
To me, being a good team member is all about constant communication, transparency, and leaving your ego at the door. Whether you are stepping up as a Scrum Master to help clear out roadblocks, or you're a developer just grinding through your daily tasks, everyone has to be on the exact same page with the "Definition of Done." It really just comes down to collaborating actively, respecting the sprint schedule, and remembering that the ultimate goal is to cross the finish line together with a working product, rather than just finishing your own isolated assignments and checking out.
